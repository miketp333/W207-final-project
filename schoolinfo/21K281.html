Our Insights

What’s Special

Students put out an award-winning newspaper

The Downside

Some kids complain of fights

IS 281, open to children from across District 21, has talent programs in music (both vocal and instrumental), dance, art, media, science and journalism. IS 281 also serves children who are zoned for the school. In 2015, it opened a dual language Mandarin program.

The large, well-kept school has an attractive auditorium, high-quality science labs, a TV studio and a renovated dance studio. Children take classes in their talents three or four times a week.

Journalism students write, edit and publish the student newspaper, New Image, printed in the school's own printing shop. The newspaper has repeatedly won awards from the American Scholastic Press Association.

In the STEM talent, (Science, Technology, Engineering and Math), students learn Computer Assisted Design and Google Sketchup. They also create video games, apps and their own websites.

In science, 6th graders study astronomy, 7th graders forensics and 8th graders physics.

In the media talent, 6th graders learn photography and animation, 7th graders study the history of television and 8th graders make their own productions.

Students must wear white shirts and black pants. A black or white sweatshirt ,may be added in the winter.

Students in the honors track, called the Cavallaro Scholar's Academy, may take Integrated Algebra Regents and the Living Environment Regents as well as the Foreign Language Proficiency Exam.

We visited during an evening open house, and the science teacher got on well with the kids and parents. He had a great sense of humor and made everything sound like fun. The STEM teacher was enthusiastic and proud of the video his students had produced. The media talent teacher had students running the cameras for her presentation. The dancers presentation was impressive. Girls of all different shapes and sizes worked together smoothly, each getting a chance to be up in front of the stage.

Students say the school is generally safe, but there are some complaints of fights, according to the Learning Environment Survey.

The school has lost enrollment in recent years, and the Department of Education decided to open a new elementary school, Coney Island Prep Charter School in the building in 2014, over the objections of Cavallaro parents.

Admissions: Students may take the District 21 "Mark Twain" talent test or enter the school as zoned students. Students who enter the school through the test get automatically placed into their chosen talent. Students who come through the zoned program can pick 3 talents, one of which will become their major. (Eliana Mascio, November 2013; updated dual language 2015)
