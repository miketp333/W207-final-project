The Downside

Entrance, equipped with metal detectors, feels unfriendly.

The Urban Assembly Academy for Future Leaders, IS 286, is the new name for two small middle schools in the same building that were merged in 2016: the Urban Assembly Institute for New Technologies and the Renaissance Leadership Academy. Melisha Jackman, who had been principal of the Renaissance Leadership Academy, remains as principal of the newly formed school.

The J-shaped brick building housing the school was originally Manhattanville Middle School but is now better known as the Adam Clayton Powell Jr. Education Complex. The school shares the campus with the Academy for Social Action (serving grades 9-12), the Urban Assembly School for the Performing Arts (grades 9-12) and Success Academy Northwest Charter School, (a middle school). The schools share an auditorium, basement lunchroom, gyms and a small outdoor recess area carved out of what used to be a faculty parking lot. (Pamela Wheaton, web reports, August 2016)
