The Downside

Some kids worry that students don't respect one another

Located in a middle class neighborhood, JHS 185 is a racially diverse school with interesting community partnerships including a cultural exchange with a classroom in China, through the Asia Society, and Spanish and Mandarin Chinese language instruction.

Seventh graders explore health careers and eighth graders focus on social justice according to the schools website. Sixth and 7th graders participate in Yo-Yo Mas Silk Road Connection project, weaving together the arts, history and architecture into a year-long study of the Silk Road [see a culmination of the project in the video at right]. Top students may take demanding Regents-level Earth Science and Algebra in the 8th grade.

Most classes, except for those labeled SP (Special Placement), or honors, mix children of different abilities. Those who are struggling in math get five extra lessons a week in classes of 8 to 10 students.Team-taught classes mix children in general education and those with special needs so they can learn from one another.

An iZone school, JHS 185 has laptops and a MOUSE squad, and yet slightly over half the students reportthat they rarely if ever complete tasks or assignments on computers in school according tothe Learning Environment Survey . In 2013 the school became a Software Engineering Pilot program: in addition to teacher-training, this program is designed to teach kids computer programming, electronics, web design, e-textiles, robotics, animation and mobile computing.

Some kids worry that students lack respect for each other according to the same Survey.

Former assistant principal Theresa Mshar, a graduate of Queens College, became principal in 2012.

Admissions: Neighborhood school. See the schools informative website for more information and to read children's writing samples.(Lydie Raschka, updated with web reports and DOE statistics, October 2013)
