Our Insights

What’s Special

Lots of field trips, gifted program and dual language classes

The Downside

Mistrust between principal and staff

Just a few blocks from Columbia University, PS 165 teaches the children of graduate students and university professors, long-time residents of the neighborhood and some new immigants from the Dominican Republic. 

PS 165 is a zoned neighborhood school that also has a gifted program, starting in kindergarten, and dual language classes, where lessons are taught in English one day and Spanish the next. These classes are designed to mix native Spanish speakers and native English speakers. About two-thirds of the school’s population is Latino, including some newcomers from the Dominican Republic.

PTA co-secretary Sharon Chisom praises the "hands-on guitar classes, the vibrant art and music teachers," and the kiln where children learn to make pottery. She said the school won a grant to develop the "The Leader in Me" program for developing social and emotional learning.

Test scores have been rising in recent years, and children grades 3, 4 and 5 score well above the citywide average. (Test scores for the middle school, however, are well below average, bringing down the scores for the school as a whole. The district superintendent is considering phasing out grades 6 to 8, leaving the school serving grades PK-5, district officials said.)  

Built in 1898, PS 165 features soaring ceilings, ample natural light, two indoor gyms and two outdoor playgrounds as well as meandering narrow halls, a too-small cafeteria, and other architectural quirks.

We visited the school on a day when classes were not in session, but the pre-kindergarten teacher we met was particularly enthusiastic and welcoming. Children may learn about plants in the classroom, then dig and grow plants themselves outside in a garden. Children take frequent field trips to places such as the local fire station, police station, a farmers’ market, the America Museum of Natural History and the Bronx Botanic Garden. Children study the stained glass windows at nearby St. John’s Cathedral as part of an art project.

Although the administrators and teachers we met at an open house seem to work together as a team, one-third of the teachers who responded to a survey say they mistrust the principal and believe she is an ineffective leader. Fewer than half the children who live in the school attendance zone attend PS 165; the rest go to charters schools and other schools of choice.

PS 165 shares a building with Mott Hall II middle school, which has its own administration and is separate from the upper grades of PS 165.

ADMISSIONS: Neighborhood elementary school. District 3 children may apply to the gifted program or to the dual-language program. (Clara Hemphill, open house, June 2017)
