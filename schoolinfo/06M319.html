Our Insights

What’s Special

Parents may observe classes, science focus

The Downside

Below average test scores

January 2015 update: MS 319 has received $25,000 in federal grants funding to create Spanish dual language program beginning September 2015.

June 2014 review: MS 319 shares a modern, cheery building on a quiet street with MS 324 and KIPP Academy Washington Heights. Ysidro Abreu, principal of the school since its founding in 2004, taught math for many years. The school has a focus on math and science, and children take part in hands-on activities such as raising trout in a classroom aquarium.

Once a month, parents are invited to a parent learning walk, in which the principal and the parent coordinator discuss the elements of good instruction, then let parents observe their kids in the classroom, Chalkbeat reports. Parents note such things as whether students do most of the talking in class and whether they asked for help when they got stuck.

I love this model because it gives parents a continuity of purposeand the tools they need to become true partners in their childrens education, schools chancellor Carmen Farina was quoted as saying.

The school has a large number of children who are learning English, including many from the Dominican Republic. Abreu, born in New York City to Dominican parents and raised in both the United States and the Dominican Republic, has a family history that helps him relate to the children. The school has below average test scores, in part because so many children are still learning the language.

The Childrens Aid Society offers after school activities for all children in the building, including a program in which children learn to repair bicycles. Childrens Aid also offers evening classes for parents and healthcare for children, including a dentist, a nurse practitioner and social worker, the UFT magazine reports.

Admissions:District 6 choice. (Clara Hemphill, web reports, June 2014)
