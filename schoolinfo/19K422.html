Our Insights

What’s Special

Beautiful new building

The Downside

Far from nearest subway

Housed in a brand new building, Spring Creek Community School features state-of-the-art science and computer labs, an art room, a music room and 600-seat auditorium for student performances. It opened with a class of 6th graders in fall 2012 and will add a grade each year until it serves grades 6-12.

According to the Department of Education middle school directory, the school plans to integrate arts and technology into regular classes. To build teacher-student relationships, each day includes an advisory period. There is a partnership with Urban Arts, a community organization that offers photography, art, theater and dance. Students will conduct science experiments in a nearby nature preserve, according to the middle school directory.

The space is shared with a D75 school and the Young Academy for Writers, an established high school. The building was designed in consultation with the schools' leaders and each school has its own floors, art rooms and science labs. There is a well-stocked shared library, with an area reserved for college research and resources, a double-sized gymnasium, a workout center that can hold an entire class and two cafeterias, according to the principal of the Young Academy for Writers.

A downside: The school is far from the nearest subway.

Admissions: District 19. Priority to students who attend an open house, information session or school fair.  (Anna Schneider, August 2012, compiled from Department of Education report [PDF])
