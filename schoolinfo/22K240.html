The Downside

Some discipline concerns

A large, traditional neighborhood school, the Andries Hudde School has three gifted programs that have long attracted students from across District 22. The school also serves children who are assigned to Hudde because they live in the school zone.

The schools enrollment has steadily decreased in recent years. There are discipline concerns: some students complain of fights and bullying, and 40 percent say they dont feel safe in bathrooms or hallways, according to the Learning Environment Survey. Teachers, too, complain that order and discipline are not maintained. The school has instituted a program called Positive Behavior Intervention Supports to improve discipline.

Children in the gifted programs take part in the Johns Hopkins math program, in which students work independently, at their own pace. That means some students may move ahead of their peers, while those who are struggling get the help they need. Some children finish high school algebra in 8th grade. Advanced students may also take Regents Earth Science and Living Environment courses. The school has an orchestra and band.

Special education: The school offers self-contained special education classes as well as team-teaching classes.

Admissions: Children who are zoned for the school are automatically admitted. Children in district 22 may apply to the gifted programs. The three gifted programs are the Center for the Intellectually Gifted (CIG), the magnet program, and the NOVA program for children who just miss the cut off for CIG and magnet. Admission to CIG is determined bya combination of factors making up a "composite score"the final 4th grade report card counts for 40 percent, state tests 40 percent, attendance ten percent and student performance measures ten percent.(Clara Hemphill, DOE statistics, middle school directory, September 2013; admissions updated January 2015)
