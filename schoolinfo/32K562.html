What’s Special

New principal creating positive tone & safe, respectful atmosphere

The Downside

Too soon to tell

Evergreen Middle School for Urban Exploloration opened in 2012 with the idea that children learn best when given the opportunity to explore their urban environment through fieldwork and other hands-on activities, according to the District 32 middle school directory. 

Students get involved in fieldwork, experiments and group projects. They develop leadership skills in regular small group advisory sessions, and have afterschool and weekend activities, including remediation for those who need it.

Lauren Reiss became principal in June 2013. She was formerly principal at IS 166 in the Bronx and, briefly, at JHS 80, also in the Bronx. In a February 2014 telephone conversation she said that "safety and respect are taken very seriously" at the school and that it is known as a safe haven in the neighborhood. She pointed out that the Learning Environment Survey (cited in InsideStats on this page) lags by a year and reflects data under the previous principal. "We're creating a kid-centered environment. Bullying is not tolerated," she said.

Students wear a school uniform of a polo shirt and khaki pants or skirts. After school activities include boys and girls basketball and co-ed volleyball. Students are required to do community service.

The school replaces IS 296, [the building shown in the photo from the DOE's website] which is closing in 2014 due to poor performance, and shares the building with the Achievement First Bushwick Charter School.

Special education: About one-quarter of the students receive special education services in either self-contained or team-teaching classes.

Admissions:  Evergreen is a zoned school, but all District 32 students may apply. Priority goes to District 32 students who attend an open house, tour or district fair. (Pamela Wheaton web reports, June 2013; updated February 2014)
