Our Insights

What’s Special

Principal reaches out to parents

The Downside

Academic achievement is low

IS 151 is a small middle school that shares a building with PS/IS 31 and the well-regarded KIPP Academy Charter School. Built in the 1970s, the building has a drab brown-brick exterior and a windswept blacktop and concrete yard in front. Inside, the building is nothing fancy, but it's clean and well-kept. The presence of elementary school children and the high-achieving KIPP kids helps contribute to a calm tone.

Principal Soccoro Rivera “has tried her best to reach out to the parents by encouraging enrollment in the PTA, with ‘parent's night out’ free dinners and Thanksgiving potluck dinners,” a parent wrote Insideschools.

In some respects, IS 151 represents the downside of school choice: successful charter schools and higher performing district schools have attracted the most motivated and successful students, and IS 151 has disproportionate numbers of kids who struggle both academically and socially.

Special education:  Nearly 30 percent of the students at IS 151 receive special education services, a proportion that has grown in recent years as enrollment has declined.

Admissions:  District 7 choice. (Clara Hemphill, May 2014)
