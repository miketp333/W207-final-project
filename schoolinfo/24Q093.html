Our Insights

What’s Special

Dual language honors program

The Downside

High suspension rate

IS 93 is a large, zoned neighborhood school in the Ridgewood section of Queens. A highlight of the school is its dual language honors programs, designed to teach Spanish to English-speakers. Students in the program spend 70 percent of their day speaking English and 30 percent of the day speaking Spanish, according to the schools website.

The school has a high rate of suspensions: more than 240 children were suspended in 2011, including more than 50 who received superintendents suspensions for serious infractions. One-third of the teachers responding to the Learning Environment Survey said order and discipline are not maintained and nearly one-third of the students said they do not feel safe.

Special education: Nearly 200 students receive special education services, either in "self-contained" classes or in team-taught classes that mix special needs- with general education students.

Admissions: Neighborhood school. (Clara Hemphill, DOE data, May 2012)
