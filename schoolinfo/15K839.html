What’s Special

New middle school in new building near Prospect Park

The Downside

Too soon to tell

Middle school students explore nearby Prospect Park and take part in lots of hands-on projects at MS 839, a new middle school that opened in 2015 in District 15 with 100 6th-graders.

MS 839 shares a newly constructed building with grades 3-5 of nearby PS 130 and with a District 75 school. Each school has its own science lab and shares a full gymnasium, a combination gym and auditorium and an outdoor playground. At full growth, MS 839 will enroll some 350 students in grades 6-8. [Image of the new building from the school's website.]

Developed with input from the community and parents, the school recruited students from throughout the district to reflect its diverse neighborhoods. In an interview with DNAinfo, founding director Michael Perlberg said that prospective families and community members told the founders that they wanted a middle school with a "hands-on" or "project-based" curriculum. MS 839 will "get students out into the community and connect their learning to the real world," he said. Perlberg himself is an avid cyclist who helped design the use of bike and pedestrian lanes at Prospect Park. Sports and expeditionary learning are key components of the new school,according to the school's website.

Perlberg has spent his education career at the Bushwick School of Social Justice, most recently as an assistant principal. He also serves as an advisor and teacher trainer for the Teaching Fellows program and as coordinator of that program at City College and Brooklyn College. Attention is paid to students' emotional as well as academic development, he told DNAinfo. Students attend daily advisories where they meet in small groups with an advisor who keeps track of how they are doing inside and outside of their classes.

Admissions: Lottery.District 15 priority.(Pamela Wheaton, web reports, March 2015)
