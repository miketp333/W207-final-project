Our Insights

The Academy for Personal Leadership and Excellence opened in September, 2010 to replace MS 399, which closed in June 2011. Founding principal, Angelo Ledda developed the new school while serving as principal for MS 399. Ledda arrived at MS 399 in 2007, a year before the Department of Education announced its phase-out, but long after the school had developed a poor record of achievement.

"When the phase-out was announced, [MS 399] had a very active student council," Ledda said explaining his chosen themes of personal leadership and excellence. "That was a success and I wanted to bring that to the new school." In addition to the student council, students fulfill community service requirements each year. Starting in the 7th grade, students study Spanish. In 8th grade they take Regents courses in algebra and earth science.

The school shares its space with the East Fordham Academy for the Arts. The building boasts a renovated swimming pool and a fitness center equipped with treadmills and exercise bicycles.

Special Education: This school hosts an ASD Nest Program for children with autism spectrum disorders (ASDs). Students with ASD learn in a classroom alongside typically developing children, taught by two teachers who have been trained in the program’s specialized curriculum and teaching strategies.

Admissions:  Priority to students zoned for the MS 399 building.  (Laura Zingmond, telephone interview, March 2010; updated with information about the Nest Program by Pauline Zaldonis, August 2013)
