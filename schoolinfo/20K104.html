P.S./I.S. 104 The Fort Hamilton School sits in a vibrant commercial and residential section of Bay Ridge, a few blocks away from the Verrazano Bridge and the Fort Hamilton army base. It is a large school, with strong connections to the community that has roots in the school's more than 100-year history.

Marie DiBella has been the principal of P.S./I.S. 104 since 1996 and has created a welcoming place for her students, in her own words, "a neighborhood place" for students who are newly arrived immigrants, children of longtime residents, from military families at the fort, or have learning disabilities.

Students are encouraged to contribute to charities and do community service in the school. When we visited, girls in the lunchroom were stamping books for the school library, while a group of "Buddies" from the higher grades was getting ready to tutor younger kids in the classroom. Kids also act as peer moderators. At Thanksgiving the school donates gift baskets to needy families. The sense of school community is strengthened by school wide spirit days and the Book of the Month, where students at all grade levels read the same book and create projects around it.

Classes at P.S/I.S. 104 are not tracked; they contain students with a wide range of abilities. The key, DiBello explains, is grouping students by ability and interest within their classroom. Projects are assigned the same way.

A 1st grade class we visited was learning spelling and writing in a hands-on way. Students enthusiastically tapped out syllables, drew letters in colored sand, and sounded out words. An advantage of the appraoch is that it seems to work well for a wide spectrum of students including English language learners.

The school has two science labs, a computer room, and a large, recently renovated schoolyard. Spanish is taught to students in the 6th to 8th grade.

Because of its large student population, the school is housed in two separate buildings several blocks apart. The main building contains grades 1 through 8 and also includes a District 75 program. The school's annex, the Fort Hamilton Early Childhood Center, has four kindergarten classes, one 1st grade class, and a special education 2nd grade class. The annex is presently shared with P.S. 264 Bay Ridge Elementary School for the Arts, which is slated to move to another building by September 2012.

Admission: Zoned students are automatically admitted into P.S./I.S. 104. The school will take students outside the zone if seats are available, but there are usually not many seats available. A limited number of seats are open for incoming 6th graders.Admission to the selective middle school "Superintendent's Program"is determined by a combination of factors making up a "composite score". The final 4th grade report card counts for 45 percent, state tests 45 percent, attendance five percent and punctuality five percent.

Special education: Most special education students are mainstreamed. There are 12: 1 classes in both the main building and the annex. The annex is handicapped accessible. The main building is not handicapped accessible.

Sports and clubs: An extended day program is available on Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday afternoons for students who need extra help. After school Thursday programs include Lego robotics, dance, and sports (including CHAMPS sports).

High school: Most graduates go to Fort Hamilton High School. Others go to Br ooklyn Technical High School, Staten Island Technical High School, Bard High School Early College, High School of Telecommunication Arts and Technology, and private or parochial schools. (Eliana Mascio, March 2012; admissions updated January 2015)
