Our Insights

What’s Special

An orderly and supportive atmosphere

The Downside

Instruction could be more challenging

IS 10 is a neighborhood middle school that is housed in an historic four-story building in Astoria. The school has a safe and orderly atmosphere, according to the Learning Environment Survey, and parents report feeling satisfied with the education their children receive at the school.

IS 10 is led by Clemente Lopes, who has been the principal since 2005. Clemente gets positive reviews from teachers, the majority of whom report that he is an effective manager who understands how children learn, according to the Learning Environment Survey.

Students say that they are expected to work hard and that extra help is available when they need it. “My teachers care if I learn,” one student said in the school’s Quality Review. However, the Quality Review also reports that some teachers struggle to engage students with different levels of ability, leaving some high-performing students bored.

Students at IS 10 wear uniforms that consist of a polo shirt and khaki pants. The school website states that any student not wearing khaki pants or gym pants will be given green sweat pants to wear for the day.

The school has a partnership with the Relationship Abuse Prevention Program (RAPP), which leads workshops about bullying, sexual harassment, communication, and healthy relationships. The Beacon program offers a variety of activities for students after school. School sports include basketball, volleyball, and tennis, according to the Middle School Directory.

English language learners: Some English language learning students are taught in self-contained classes led by ESL teachers, while others are integrated into classes with their English-speaking peers.

Special education: IS 10 offers Special Education Teacher Support Services (SETSS), Collaborative Team Teaching (CTT) classes, and self-contained classes for students with special needs.

Admissions: IS 10 is a zoned middle school with a magnet program that accepts students from across District 30. (Pauline Zaldonis, web reports, September 2013)
