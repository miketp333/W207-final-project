Our Insights

What’s Special

School for children with disabilities has supportive Pre-K

The Downside

Little information available

PS 168 has a Pre-K program that is extremely supportive and helpful in aiding parents to support their childs learning, surveys say. The quality of teaching is good, according to the 2015 Quality Review.

The school, which serves 530 students from Pre-K to grade 12, is located in 7 sites across the Bronx. The average attendance rate for the 2014-2015 school year was 85.7 percent, according to the Comprehensive Education Plan.

Admissions: Citywide. PS 168 is part of District 75, a network of 69 schools dedicated to New York Citys students with disabilities who require increased attention. District 75 provides educational, vocational, and behavioral support programs for students who are on the autism spectrum, have significant cognitive delays, are significantly emotionally challenged, have a sensory impairment or have multiple disabilities. (Katie Safter & Hugo Segal, web reports, August 2017)
