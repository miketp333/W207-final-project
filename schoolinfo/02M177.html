Our Insights

What’s Special

New middle school on the Upper East Side with inclusive admissions

The Downside

Too soon to tell

A small middle school that opened on the Upper East Side with a 6th grade in 2014, MS 177 admits half of its incoming students by screened admissions (grades, scores, recommendations, attendance, punctuality and group interview) and half by lottery (limited unscreened, meaning those who attended an info-session get priority).Students attend all classes together and class size is kept small, between 18-22. It shares a large building, built in 1898, with PS 158.

The founding principal is Christina Riggio, a Teach for America alum and a graduate of Columbia University's Teachers College. She was a middle school social studies teacher in the Bronx.

See the school's website for more information.

Admissions: District 2 choice based on the formula described above.(Pamela Wheaton, Web reports, February 2015)
