The Downside

Overcrowded

PS 71 is a large old-fashioned neighborhood school that serves generations from the same family, some newcomers and a good-size population of children with special needs. The school is crowded and class size is above average, yet most teachers report discipline and order are maintained according to school surveys.

Former assistant principal Phyllis Calzolaio became principal in 2011. Under her leadership, test scores, safety and order have improved. She and her staff were able to move the school from "developing" on the city's quality review, to "proficient," over a two-year span. They did this by offering more workshops and coaching to teachersthe school has three math coaches and a literacy coachand instilling a more positive atmosphere.

An Anti-Bullying committee meets monthly to evaluate the school culture. There are monthly themes like friendship, respect and gratitude, as well as assemblies promoting school spirit and respect for all. The principal encourages students to make PS 71 their "happy place" according to the city's Quality Review, and she stands outside to greet them in the morning. Deans and a guidance counselor are on hand for support. Calzolaio gets high marks from teachers on school surveys: Ninety-six percent say she is an effective manager.

The school makes a concerted effort to bring the parents into the building with events ranging from parent coffee klatches to family math night and reading night. They are invited to the annual art expo and the science fair.

The school offers math and science Regents classes, Spanish instruction and preparation for the specialized high school exam. Middle school students take trips to upstate New York, Boston and Washington, D.C.

After school sports and clubs for students in grades 4-8 include basketball, volleyball, fitness, cheerleading, wrestling, art, band, violin, robotics, Italian, dance and drama. There are full-time art, music and drama teachers. Cooking is offered to children in grades 5-8.

Special education: The school has four small self-contained classes, and ICT (integrated-co-teaching) classes on every grade.

Admissions: Neighborhood school.(Lydie Raschka, web reports and school data, August 2014)
