Our Insights

What’s Special

Art classes working toward portfolio development in 8th grade

The Downside

Safety and respect could be improved

Since its opening in 2007, the Middle School for Art and Philosophy has struggled to overcome a reputation as being a choatic, unsafe school under poor management. Under new leadership since 2011, test scores are inching up, and satisfaction from parents and teachers has improved, despite increased numbers of children with special needs.

Students take philosophy classes with a focus on critical thinking, argument and debate. During three years of art, they develop and present a portfolio in the 8th grade. Samples of artwork can be seen on the school's simple, well-organized website.

Principal Neil McNeill took the helm in 2011 when founding principal Andrew Buck was removed from his post according to an article in the New York Daily News. McNeill was a teacher at IS 252, the school formerly housed in the Lenox Road building, which closed in 2009 due to poor performance. The building also houses Kings Collegiate Charter School. [volunteers from New York Cares spruce up the outdoor space with bulb plantings and a mural in photo at right]

On school surveys students complain that there is still fighting at the school and over half say kids don't treat each other with respect, but teachers report they are mostly pleased with the new leadership.

Students may participate in step, dance, martial arts, soccer or basketball.

Admissions: Neighborhood school. Priority goes to Brooklyn students. (Lydie Raschka, November 2013)
