What’s Special

The Downside

Too soon to say

Students learn by doing, at Van Siclen Community Middle School, tackling projects based on real world issues. Opened in 2013 with 6th grade only, the school will add one grade per year through 8th grade. Every student in the school is assigned a mentor.

Teachers receive guidance from trainers at EduChange, an organization that works with schools to provide practical assistance and strategies. As the school expands plans are to offer advanced Regent’s algebra, living environment and Spanish classes. 

A graduate of Brooklyn College, principal Adonna McFarland taught at PS 309 and Walt Whitman Middle School. She is a graduate of the NYC Leadership Academy, the city’s training ground for principals.

A special feature of Van Siclen is the media and video production program. Children learn to write and produce videos about topics that are meaningful to them with guidance from the Urban Wall Street Project/Young Producers program.

Sports include tennis and basketball. Children are required to do community service projects and wear uniforms.   

The school is located in a building shared with IS 166, which is being phased out and replaced after years of poor performance. No new students were admitted in September 2013, and the phase-out will be completed in June 2015.  

Admissions: Limited unscreened. Priority goes to district 19 students who demonstrate an interest by participating in an information session, on a school tour and/or at an open house. (Lydie Raschka, web reports, October 2013)
