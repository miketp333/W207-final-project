The Downside

Discipline sometimes ends up in heated arguments.

NOVEMBER 2004 REVIEW: IS 303 is a large, traditional school that is experimenting with some unconventional approaches -- and having some success in its efforts. Each grade is overseen by an assistant principal with many years of experience at the school, and 6th and 7th grade classes are "self-contained," meaning that students do not travel to different rooms for different subjects. One assistant principal said that students and classroom teachers develop a sense of ownership of their rooms, and that test scores are improving as a result. The administration is now considering a similar reorganization of the 8th grade.

In the classrooms, teachers are using the city's progressive curriculum. There are libraries of books in the rooms and students work on assignments in groups -- two signature features of the city approach. One 7th grade teacher devised a clever way for students to quietly get her attention for help while she circulates through the room. All groups have a stack of colored plastic cups: when they need help, they place a red one on top; when they've completed an assignment, a green cup goes up.

Teachers also attend class themselves. The school has designated a classroom where they work with a math coach once a week to hone their skills in teaching according to the city's mandated math program. Among other things, they learn how to encourage students to use "manipulatives" -- tangible objects such as blocks, tiles, and fake coins -- to help them understand concepts.

The school offers "Sigma" programs -- District 21's gifted and talented classes -- in digital media, law, and dance. There are a dance studio, classroom with a jury box, and well-outfitted computer labs to accommodate the classes. In a digital media class, groups of students were shooting silent short films with camcorders and learning to edit them on computers. They also learned how to use the Adobe Photoshop image software.

Eighth graders can take Regents prep classes in Math A and earth science, and the school is proud of their success: some 96 percent of the students who took it passed the Regents science test and fully 100 percent passed the math exam in June 2004.

Like most middle schools dealing with adolescent angst, IS 303 faces misbehavior from students from time to time. Deans are generally responsible for handling student discipline. On our visit, we observed an argument between a student and a dean that got a little too loud and heated. According to an assistant principal, one of the challenges in running a middle school is to "keep [the building] safe," but says that so far the IS 303 has been fairly successful.

Tutoring is offered after school, which an administrator says about "300 kids come to for help." There is also an after school program targeted to help students learning English.

Admissions: Students applying to the Sigma program are expected to take a test and audition. (Catherine Man, November 2004)
