Our Insights

What’s Special

English classes for adults and children

The Downside

Overcrowded

A gigantic school with a large immigrant population, IS 61 offers English as a Second Language to both children and their parents. Children take classes during the day, while adults may take ESL or GED classes in the evening.

We extend learning outside the classroom because its not just for the student, it can also be for the parent, Principal Joseph Lisa said in an interview with Schoolbook.

Lisa, who took the helm in July 2007 after 10 years as assistant principal, has divided the school into five academies, each with its own assistant principal and guidance counselor, to give it a small-school feel.

When people come here, although we are one of the largest schools running on a single session, the feel isnt that big, Lisa told Schoolbook. You always have people in the hallway. Its clogged but its reduced into sections through the building. Students stay in the same academy for three years, allowing them to develop closer relationships with teachers and staff. They see the dean, assistant principal through the years. All this continuity over the course of time, it assists the student, Lisa said.

The tone of the school is energized but peaceful, according to the Department of Educations Quality Review. Students have the opportunity to take drama and art, the review said.

The 2013 opening of a new middle school, The Corona Arts and Sciences Academy or MS 311, eased overcrowding at IS 61, but even with the new school, IS 61 is packed.

Special education: There are Special Education Teacher Support Services (SETSS), Collaborative Team Teaching (CTT) classes, and self-contained classes for students with special needs only.

Admissions: Neighborhood school. (Clara Hemphill, web reports, September 2013)
