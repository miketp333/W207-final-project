The Downside

Students complain of fights and bullying

JHS 123 serves a needy population, including many children who are learning English as a Second Language and many who need special education services. The school is part of the Middle School Quality Initiative, which gives intensive extra reading help to children who are behind in their studies.

Children complain of fights and bullying and many teachers say the principal is not an effective manager, according to the Learning Environment Survey. Fewer than half the teachers surveyed said they would recommend the school to parents.

On the positive side, some students are part of the Gilder Lehrman Center for American Studies, which offers class trips to Medieval Times, Philadelphia and Washington, D.C., as a way to get children excited about studying history.

The school shares a building with the School for Inquiry and Social Justice.

Admissions: Zoned school. Preference for the Gilder Lehrman program goes to District 8 students who attend an open house. (Clara Hemphill, web reports, October 2013)
