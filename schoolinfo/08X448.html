Our Insights

What’s Special

Small class size and a nurturing environment

The Downside

Low attendance rates

Soundview Academy for Culture and Scholarship is a small, nurturing middle school located on one floor of the MS 131 building. Founding Principal William Frackelton, a 15-year veteran NYC public schools teacher and a graduate of the Aspiring Principals Program, and his energetic staff have created a culture of order and respect at Soundview. The school strives to support and challenge students of all levels, and while most students come in with below-average academic skills, the majority are ready for high school by the time they graduate and do well in their 9th-grade classes.

Classes at Soundview are small, and students get lots of one-on-one attention. Students start and finish each school day by checking in with an advisory group of 10-15 students and a faculty member typically of the same gender. Students also meet in their advisory groups three times each week in scholars class, where they discuss topics like study skills and bullying. We sweat the small stuff here, William Frackelton told us during our visit, emphasizing that the teachers and staff at Soundview work hard to create a positive school environment, although the school still struggles with attendance rates well below the citywide average.

Teachers at Soundview do a good job teaching students with different levels of ability. They aim not just to remediate, but to accelerate their students, according to Frackelton. Students have access to individualized online lessons as part of the citys iZone initiative. Teachers use a mix of worksheets and hands-on activities in their lessons. In the Regents-level algebra class we visited, students worked in small groups to carry out transformations, graphing the rotations of various shapes on a grid. In science class, students go on field trips to places like the Bronx Zoo and the American Museum of Natural History through the Urban Advantage program. Interested students may also participate in Mentoring in Medicine, which runs an afterschool program in which students interact with health professionals to learn about their careers.

Students looking for extra academic support or an academic challenge may participate in the schools Saturday Academy or its Club Reset afterschool program to get extra homework help. Two Regents-level classes are offered: algebra and living environment. Frackelton plans to add more Regents-level classes in the coming years in addition to other electives (such as forensics) to give students the opportunity to take more challenging classes. The school also has a dual language program that allows students to become proficient in English and Spanish.English language learners may also participate in the dual language program, in which students speak English and Spanish on alternating days.

Soundview has a strong arts program, offering musical theater, visual arts, and film. Thanks to a partnership with Arts Connection and the Broadway Junior Musical Theater Program, the school puts on a musical theater performance every year. The newly renovated library includes a soundproof room, and MacBook Airs with movie-making software. The school also participates in the Carnegie Hall Count Me In program, which helps 8th-grade students prepare for auditions to specialized high schools.

Extracurricular activities include basketball, cheerleading, step team, soccer, and flag football. The YMCA runs an afterschool program at the school Monday - Friday.

Special education: There are both self-contained and Integrated Co-Teaching (ICT) classes, which mix children with special needs and those in general education, as well as Special Education Teacher Support Services (SETSS). Frackelton told us that the school does a good job moving students from self-contained classes to ICT or general education classes.

Admissions: District 8. Preference is given to those who visit the school or attend a middle school fair. (Pauline Zaldonis, February 2014)
